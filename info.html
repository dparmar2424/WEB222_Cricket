A brief History of Cricket:

Cricket is a popular team sport that originated in England in the 16th century. The game has since spread to many other parts of the world, particularly in countries that were part of the British Empire. The earliest known reference to cricket dates back to the late 16th century, and the game was popularized in the 18th century with the formation of the Marylebone Cricket Club (MCC) in London. The MCC became the official governing body of the sport and established the rules that are still used today.

The popularity of cricket continued to grow throughout the 20th century, with many countries establishing their own cricket boards and participating in international competitions. The World Cup, a major international cricket tournament, was first held in 1975 and has since become one of the most prestigious events in the sport. Today, cricket is played at both amateur and professional levels, with international matches and tournaments being watched by millions of fans around the world. It remains a popular sport in countries such as India, Pakistan, Australia, England, and the West Indies.

  • Toss:

    • Before the start of the match, the two captains meet at the center of the pitch for a coin toss. The winning captain chooses whether to bat or bowl first

  • Innings:

    • The team that wins the toss chooses to bat or bowl first. The team batting first is called the "innings" and their aim is to score as many runs as possible.

  • Bowling:

    • The team bowling first aims to take wickets (dismissals) and prevent the batting team from scoring runs. The bowler delivers the ball to the batsman, who tries to hit the ball and score runs.

  • Fielding:

    • The fielding team aims to stop the ball and prevent the batting team from scoring runs. Fielders are positioned around the field in different positions.

  • Change of innings:

    • After the first team completes their innings, the teams switch roles. The team that was bowling now gets to bat, and the team that was batting now gets to bowl.

  • Scoring:

    • Each time the batsman hits the ball and runs to the other end of the pitch, they score one run. If the ball crosses the boundary of the field, the batting team scores four runs (a "boundary").     • The match is over when both teams have completed their innings. The team that scores the most runs is declared the winner.
